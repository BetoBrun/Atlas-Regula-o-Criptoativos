Why this Atlas matters

This project is not a simple repository of laws. It is an analytical observatory designed to compare how jurisdictions allocate power between the State, the market, and digital financial infrastructure.

The central question is not merely whether a country regulates crypto-assets, but how it regulates exchanges, stablecoins, tokenization, and public digital money, and what these choices imply for innovation, investor protection, legal certainty, and institutional concentration.

Methodology Thematic pillars Jurisdictions

Methodology

The Atlas adopts a comparative legal-institutional approach. It evaluates not only the existence of rules, but also their architecture, coherence, and distributive effects on the digital financial ecosystem.

Core question

How do different jurisdictions structure crypto regulation, and what does this reveal about supervision, competition, innovation, and the centralization of digital monetary infrastructure?

Units of analysis

  • Jurisdiction
  • Normative act
  • Supervisory authority
  • Asset category and regulatory scope

Comparative criteria

  • Legal certainty
  • Innovation openness
  • Investor and user protection
  • Institutional coherence
  • Risk of excessive centralization

Stablecoins

The Atlas treats stablecoins as the main intersection between crypto regulation, payments, reserves, redemption, and financial stability. Their treatment reveals how each jurisdiction organizes private innovation under public oversight.

Tokenization

Tokenization is assessed as a regulatory gateway to the integration of digital assets with real-economy assets, capital markets, and institutional finance.

CBDC and public digital money

The Atlas distinguishes between wholesale, retail, and hybrid CBDC models and highlights how programmability, privacy, and intermediation choices affect the balance between innovation and concentration of monetary power.
